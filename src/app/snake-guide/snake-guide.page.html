So your choice of pet is a snake?? someone must've been a fan of Kaa, while your new found friend isnt going to be hypnotising anyone with its eyes, they will certainly enchant you with their charm. Snakes make a great beginner reptile for the fact they teach the basics of all reptile needs, while being a clean, low maintenance pet, they feed rarely and are very easy to handle.

the most popular beginner snakes for most are between corn snakes and western hognoses, due to their friendly temperment, small max size, and ease of husbandry, they are both burrowing snakes so you wont need to worry about having a tall enclosure and instead will value more horizontal space for the enclosure, depending on climate, you can sometimes get away with not having heat bulb, and instead can opt for a heat mat, which will save on those ever increasing power bills.
Snakes on average live anywhere from 5 to 14 years, so getting one is a big investment of time, but with the right handeling and care, one can develop a deep and personal bond.

DIET: Most snakes are pureley carnivorous, with smaller species feeding off chicks and pinkies, which should happen about once a week, and larger species may need to be fed butcher qaulity meet, or in extreme cases, other snakes.
